Nequi (PSE) is a payment method that allows Nequi users to make purchases and payments online by debiting directly from their Nequi account through the PSE button powered by ACH Colombia. This is not a direct integration with the Nequi API — it is an abstraction built on top of PSE: during the payment flow, the user selects Nequi as their payment method and authenticates in the Nequi virtual banking portal to authorize the debit.

Nequi is a digital wallet from Bancolombia with more than 25 million users in Colombia, making it one of the financial institutions with the broadest coverage in the PSE ecosystem.

## Nequi (PSE) Benefits

- **Large user base**: With more than 25 million active users, Nequi is one of the most widely used financial institutions in Colombia, expanding the reach of your business.
- **Familiar experience for the user**: Nequi users already know the PSE flow — they simply select Nequi as their payment method and authenticate through their usual portal.
- **Available 24/7**: Operating over the ACH Colombia PSE network, transactions can be completed at any time of day, every day of the year.

## Use Cases

- **Merchants targeting Nequi users**: If your customers are primarily Nequi users, this payment method offers them a familiar experience with no additional setup steps.
- **Card-free alternative**: Allows users without a credit or debit card to pay directly from their Nequi savings account or electronic deposit, reducing friction at checkout.

## Payment Method Behavior

Nequi (PSE) is a redirected payment method. The user is directed to the PSE portal where they authenticate through the Nequi virtual banking interface to authorize the debit. Once the transaction is complete, the user returns to the payment gateway and sees the final status of their transaction.

![Nequi PSE payment flow](/en/payments/payment-methods/nequi_pse.webp)

- **Integrations through**: [Webcheckout](/en/checkout), [Microsites](/en/payments/introduction-to-microsites), and [Payment Links](/en/payment-links)  
  Once the user completes the payment form, they will be redirected to the PSE portal where they will authenticate through the Nequi virtual banking interface to authorize the debit. After completing the process, they will return to the payment gateway and see the final status of their transaction.

- **Integrations through**: [Gateway](/en/gateway)  
  Gateway integrations follow an asynchronous process in which a payment session link is provided, redirecting the user to the PSE portal to complete authentication and authorize the debit from their Nequi account. Once completed, the user will return to the return URL specified in the payment session creation request.

  To obtain the final transaction status, a request must be made to the endpoint [Query transaction by internal reference](/en/gateway/api/reference/transaction#query-request).

## Restrictions

- **Requires an active Nequi account**: The user must have a Nequi account (low-value deposit or savings account) with sufficient balance to cover the transaction amount.
- **Transaction limits defined by Nequi**: Per-transaction amount limits are determined by the user's Nequi account settings. Users are advised to verify their limits with Nequi before making high-value payments.
- **Supported account types**: Debits are made exclusively from Nequi savings accounts or electronic deposits; other account types are not supported.
- **Maximum processing time**: PSE has a maximum time window for processing a transaction. If the user does not complete authentication within that time, the transaction expires.

<details>
<summary>What is the difference between Nequi (PSE) and a direct Nequi API integration?</summary>
<p>Nequi (PSE) uses the ACH Colombia PSE button as the payment channel: the user selects Nequi as their bank and authenticates through the Nequi virtual banking portal. A direct Nequi API integration (Nequi Button) works via push notifications in the Nequi app and requires a separate agreement with Nequi as a provider. Both methods charge from the user's Nequi account, but the authentication flow and technical integration are different.</p>
</details>

<details>
<summary>What happens if the transaction expires before the user completes authentication in Nequi?</summary>
<p>PSE has a maximum time window for processing a transaction. If the user does not complete the flow within that time, the transaction is marked as failed and the user must initiate a new payment attempt.</p>
</details>
